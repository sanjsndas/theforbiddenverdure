About The Forbidden Verdure

Discover the passionate team behind our mission to revolutionize gardening with natural, sustainable solutions.

Our Story

The Forbidden Verdure began as a small community garden project in 2015, when our founder, Dr. Evelyn Green, noticed the remarkable effects of bamboo ash on struggling tomato plants. What started as a simple experiment soon blossomed into a comprehensive research initiative exploring the untapped potential of natural soil amendments.

Over the years, we've grown from a backyard operation to a recognized authority in sustainable gardening practices. Our team of horticulturists, soil scientists, and passionate gardeners work tirelessly to uncover nature's secrets and share them with the gardening community.

Today, The Forbidden Verdure serves thousands of gardeners worldwide through our educational resources, workshops, and community initiatives. We remain committed to our founding principle: that the most powerful gardening solutions are often the simplest ones hidden in plain sight.

Mission & Vision

🎯

Our Mission

To empower gardeners with scientifically-backed, natural solutions that enhance plant health, improve soil vitality, and promote sustainable gardening practices worldwide.

🔭

Our Vision

A world where every gardener has access to effective, eco-friendly solutions that work in harmony with nature, reducing our collective reliance on synthetic chemicals.

Our Values

At The Forbidden Verdure, our work is guided by a set of core principles that shape everything we do:

Sustainability

We prioritize solutions that work with natural systems, not against them, ensuring our gardening practices leave the earth healthier for future generations.

Scientific Integrity

All our recommendations are based on rigorous research and testing, combining traditional wisdom with modern scientific understanding.

Accessibility

We believe that effective gardening solutions should be available to everyone, regardless of experience level or budget.

Community

We foster a collaborative environment where gardeners can share knowledge, experiences, and successes to help each other grow.

Innovation

We continuously explore new approaches and rediscover ancient techniques to solve modern gardening challenges.

Transparency

We openly share our methods, results, and even our failures, believing that honest communication builds trust and advances knowledge.

Community Impact

Beyond our research and educational efforts, The Forbidden Verdure is committed to making a tangible difference in communities through various initiatives:

School Garden Program

We partner with local schools to establish educational gardens where students learn about sustainable agriculture, nutrition, and environmental stewardship. Our bamboo ash techniques help these gardens thrive with minimal resources.

Community Workshops

Throughout the year, we offer free and low-cost workshops on topics ranging from soil health to natural pest management. These hands-on sessions empower gardeners of all skill levels.

Research Partnerships

We collaborate with universities and agricultural extension services to conduct field trials and expand the scientific understanding of natural gardening amendments.

Resource Sharing

Our online library of guides, videos, and research papers is freely available to anyone interested in sustainable gardening practices.
